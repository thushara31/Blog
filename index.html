Places Must Visit

georiga aquarim

I visited in December 2019

Great experience. The tank with the whale sharks was immense and they had a viewing window that I was told was 63 feet long and looked at least 25 feet high. This is as close as you can get to scuba diving without getting wet. The animals looked well taken care of. I did a shark and ray encounter and it was fun and educational. This is a definite must if you are anywhere neat Atlanta Georgia. One caution, all of the encounters book up early, so book your encounter well in advance. Also, the shows have limited seating. You have to register for the shows when you get there in order to get in.



cambelback mountian resort

I visited in December 2022

I have been coming to Camelback for nearly a decade now- it’s always a fun time on the mountain with friends. The trails are long and well maintained, and the lifts are nice. Staff interactions have always been awesome, even in the face of some very rude cliental. It’s clear the staff (esp at the rental desk and ski patrol ) work hard. That said - Camelback loses points on mismanaged lines, customer service, poor quality ( and expensive ) food and the audacity to charge for parking on top of an already very expensive day. They also went to some ridiculous RFID system where there is no one there to manage people getting on lifts, until you get to the actual lift. You have to pull out this little card ( hard to do all geared up) to scan to get through the equivalent of a metal detector- so in essence you que to Que- and society isnt set up for this to run smoothly - at all. Lines for lifts were approx 20 min yesterday. Granted it was a holiday week- maybe limit the passes you sell if all trails aren’t open? Please bring back scannable paper lift tickets - orange coats and a price scanners seem to do a better job of hurdling the masses This review is based on our experience Wed Dec 28. - 2.5 hours in guest services, $500 between the two of us for lift tickets , parking and rental to go on 6 runs. It was a fun day , but may be going to a resort off the beaten path in the future. Some tips- buy your passes online (ahead of your arrival, because there’s little cell phone service when it’s that crowded), bring your own gear or rent gear off mountain - there are several spots in the area, and bring your own food or wait to apres at a restaurant like Kimche and Wasabi. If you can’t buy your passes ahead of time make sure you have enough friends to wait in both lines simultaneously. Bottom line- pack your patience and realize , like everything else, the pandemic has raised costs and lowered quality and expectations.
